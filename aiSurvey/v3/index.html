Generative AI in Higher Education

Student survey — version 3 (research instrument)

Information & consent

You are invited to take part in a study about how university and college students use generative AI (GenAI) tools — such as ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, or Claude — in their studies. The survey is open to students in any field, not just computing. We ask about your programming background because it may shape how you use these tools.

  • Voluntary. Participation is voluntary. You can stop at any time by closing the page; no partial data is stored until you press Submit.
  • Anonymous. We do not ask for your name, email, or student ID. Responses are stored without direct identifiers.
  • Self-generated code. You will create a short non-identifying code so that, if you complete this survey twice (start and end of term), we can link your two responses without knowing who you are.
  • Use of data. Aggregated, de-identified data may be used in academic publications, presentations, and improvements to teaching.
  • Time. About 10–15 minutes.
  • Risks. Minimal. No questions ask about academic misconduct in a way that can be linked to you personally.
  • Contact. Questions? Contact the lecturer or the research lead listed on the course page.
